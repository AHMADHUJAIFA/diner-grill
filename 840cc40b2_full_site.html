Our Story

Two Streetcars.
One Legend.

In 1937, two Evanston streetcars were fused together on W Irving Park Rd in Lake View. What emerged wasn't just a diner — it became the heartbeat of Chicago's night.

On Christmas Eve 2016, a fire nearly ended everything. The city helped us rebuild. We reopened in 2018 — same grill, same spirit.

Our Story
A Chicago Timeline

How We Got Here

1937
Born from Streetcars

Two decommissioned Evanston streetcars were fused together on West Irving Park Road. What emerged wasn't just a diner — it was a way of life.

1960s
Chicago's Late Night Institution

Factory workers, musicians, neighborhood families. The counter was always full.

1980s–2000s
Generations of Regulars

The menu barely changed. That was the point. The Slinger. Pork chop and eggs. Bottomless coffee. Some things don't need improvement.

2016
Christmas Eve Fire

A devastating fire on December 24, 2016 threatened to end everything. But the city showed up and demanded we come back.

2018
Rebuilt. Reopened.

Same address. Same grill. Same recipes. Same soul. The regulars came back on day one — some had been waiting at the door.

Today
Still Here. Still Open.

4.6 stars. 1,076+ reviews. 87 years and counting. Open right now. The grill never stops.

2016

The Night We Almost Lost It All

Christmas Eve, 2016. A fire consumed most of the building. Within hours, the community started calling — offering support, demanding we come back.

"We weren't just going to walk away from 80 years of history. This place belongs to Chicago."
— The Diner Grill Team
Reopened 2018

Same Grill.
Stronger Soul.

The 2018 reopening preserved the original character while upgrading what needed upgrading. The streetcar DNA remains. The counter is back. The grill is hot.
